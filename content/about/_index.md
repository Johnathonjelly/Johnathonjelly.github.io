I am Johnathon Southworth, a craftsman of digital systems, weaving code, intelligence, and strategy from my base in White City, Oregon. Like the ancient philosophers who sought to understand the underlying patterns of existence, I find profound satisfaction in transforming chaotic data into crystalline insight, and lately in teaching machines themselves to perceive those patterns, building systems that serve human endeavor with both elegance and purpose.

## The Artisan's Journey

Over the past {{< years-since 2016 >}} years building software, I have forged business platforms that reveal the hidden geometries within commercial chaos. Like Daedalus crafting wings from feathers and wax, I built a manufacturing execution system that transforms the mundane (time tracking, efficiency metrics, operational data) into soaring insight that guides strategic flight, measuring true output against engineered standards. I later made it offline-first, so that even when the factory's network falls silent the work continues uninterrupted, its records kept faithfully until the connection returns. Where once there were scattered fragments of information, now there stand integrated dashboards that whisper the true pulse of industry.

## Beyond the Code

In the halls of a community college, I served as a digital augur, reading the patterns hidden within operational data and building reports that illuminated paths toward greater efficiency. Yet my most satisfying work lies in breathing life into dormant systems, implementing CRM architectures for organizations that had never before captured the rhythm of their own commerce.

Like Hermes bridging the mortal and divine realms, I have crafted sophisticated interfaces that weave together disparate systems, conjuring bidirectional flows of information where once there was only silence. Through careful integration work and webhook architectures, I transform isolated platforms into symphonies of connected intelligence, and once replaced an entire suite of costly licenses with a single platform of my own design. These endeavors appear deceptively simple yet require the patience of Sisyphus and the cunning of Odysseus to truly solve.

## The Philosopher's Code

Between the sacred acts of debugging and the ritual wrestling with database queries, I find solace in the company of ancient minds. Those philosophers and poets whose wisdom transcends millennia continue to illuminate contemporary challenges. Like the oracle at Delphi drawing insight from sacred vapors, I discover that stepping away from the digital realm often reveals the clearest path through any technical labyrinth.

My craft extends far beyond the surface layers of web development. To build a manufacturing execution system from the ground up is to reckon with resilience itself: an architecture that endures the harsh, disconnected conditions of a factory floor, holding its memory close through service workers and local stores until the network stirs again, alongside algorithms that translate raw production into the language of standards and revenue. The system spans everything from real-time data streams to the sophisticated database architecture beneath them.

More recently I have turned to teaching machines to perceive. Like Hephaestus forging automatons of gold to labor in his workshop, I have built intelligent agents that work alongside their human counterparts: a vision model, trained by my own hand and housed in a containerized service, that recognizes products from a single image; pipelines that read meaning from technical drawings as an oracle reads signs; and a conversational agent that answers dealers in natural language, tireless and ever present. Where once my software merely followed instructions, it now observes, infers, and speaks.

And when the tools of one domain proved too narrow for my vision, I set out to build a world entire. As founder of Latigo Health, I conjured a complete multi-tenant platform from nothing, governed by an immutable ledger as unalterable as the thread the Fates themselves spin. When its first foundation revealed its limits, I chose the rarer courage: to tear down what worked well enough and rebuild upon sturdier ground, choosing integrity over convenience.

The analytics platforms and CRM implementations that fill my days rest on this foundation of systems thinking. When you have built software that follows a product through every stage of its making while calculating profitability in real time, the complexities of stakeholder management and third-party integration become simply another layer in the stack.

Yet I remain eternally a student, understanding that true mastery, like the horizon, recedes as one approaches, always revealing new territories to explore.
